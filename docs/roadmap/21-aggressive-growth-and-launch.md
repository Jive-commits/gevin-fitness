# 21 — The Aggressive Growth & Launch Engine: From Rage to Repeatable

> **Thesis.** The aggressive coach is not a feature we market *around* — it **is** the marketing. In a red ocean of beautiful, identical fitness apps, polish is a tie and aggression can't be tied, because every competitor is too brand-safe to copy it. This document is the distribution engine that turns *"a fitness app called me a bitch and I cried"* into a structural top-of-funnel rivals are forbidden from entering. The rage gets us **found**; the receipts — your why, your 7:14, your fourth folded leg day — make us **undeletable**. **Stop starting over.**

Docs 19 and 20 built the wedge (the five-tier coach spectrum) and the press (the 16-concept content machine). This doc builds the **growth flywheel and launch**. Everything here is floored by a non-negotiable safety contract — and that floor is precisely what makes the edge trusted enough to ship and post.

## 1. Platform-by-platform algorithm strategy

We don't post "everywhere the same." Each platform rewards a different signal and tolerates a different controversy dial. We match the asset, the hook ratio, and the dial to the algorithm — then post native, never cross-posted with a competitor's watermark.

| Platform | Effort | Rewards | Native asset | Controversy dial | Hook lean |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | 60% | Completion, rewatch, share-to-DM | Screen-record of the voice-note **slap** (9s, real why) | **8/10** — most permissive, fastest FYP, youngest | Disbelief + Dare lead loud |
| **Reels** | 25% | Saves, sends | The **roast / streak-death card** (survives muted + cropped) | **6/10** — Meta moderates harder, audience older | Receipts + Reaction, censor more |
| **Shorts** | 10% | Watch-through, longest tail | The **climb-the-dial reaction arc** + day-7 progress | **5/10** — most conservative, ties to Google acct | Vindication + "it works" weighted |
| **X / Twitter** | 5% | The argument | Our own most divisive clips, posted by us | **owned/moderated** | The fight, on our terms |

**The rules that make the dial work:**

- **Chase shares, not likes.** A share is the forward — the entire physics of the engine. The DM-send is distribution; the like is vanity.
- **Bleep the harshest profanity in-frame.** A censor bar reads *edgier* than the uncensored word and dodges the auto-moderator — a net positive for reach and tone.
- **Post from a roster, not one account** (see §2), so the algorithm A/B tests our hooks for free.
- **X is for the argument, not the reach.** We post the divisive clips ourselves and let the discourse ("this is unhealthy" vs. "it's the only thing that's worked") manufacture the controversy PR then covers — under our own roof, where we moderate the floor in real time.

**The hard floor on every platform (non-negotiable, this is what keeps us shippable):** never a slur, never a protected class (race, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability), never sexual, never real-violence framing, never anything touching suicide, self-harm, or eating disorders. The "slap" is always a stylized avatar swinging at *the lens* — never a person — so it reads as theater, not a deepfake or a threat. The aggression aims at the **excuse**, never the person. Cross that line and you don't get a viral video; you get deplatformed.

## 2. The seeding plan — creators, not influencers

We seed **30–40 mid-tier creators (50k–500k)** across three tiers. The product roasts *them*, using *their* real onboarding why; we film the genuine flinch. **No scripts** — the authentic reaction is the asset; a scripted ad reads as an ad and dies.

| Tier | Who | Why | Dial |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Edgy fitness/comedy creators, 50k–500k | High engagement, low cost, real reactions; big enough to move installs, small enough to read as real | their own |
| **B** | Non-fitness reaction/POV creators | The mean coach is funny to people who don't lift — expands past the gym to our chronically-online beachhead | their own |
| **C** | 1–2 marquee edgy names | A launch-window spike **only after** Tier A proves the format converts | their own |

**The offer (incentives compound with performance):** a small base + **per-install or per-roast-generated bounty**, plus **early access to UNHINGED** before the public unlock — the unlock itself is their content ("I earned the mean coach"). An affiliate code closes the loop: audience installs → creator earns → creator posts variations. **The creator's payout *is* a growth loop.**

**The one-page anti-script brief:**

> *"Tell it the most honest reason you want this. Then hit record and let it come for you. Don't write jokes — the specificity is the joke. Show the dial. Show your face when the voice note plays. If it goes too far, that reaction is the best part — keep it in. And if you tell it you're hurt, film what it does next."*

**Tiered waves:**

- **Wave 1 (wk 0–2):** 10 trusted creators, exclusive early access + the locked-mode flex.
- **Wave 2 (wk 2–4):** 30 more, timed to the launch moment, 3–5 seeded posts/day.
- **Wave 3 (wk 4+):** open the **self-serve creator kit** — anyone generates their own roast, incentivized to post (early access, leaderboard, rev-share). This is where we stop *making* content and start *curating* it.

**The creator floor (protects the channel):** every seeded creator must show it's opt-in, self-directed, and that it backs off — the injury tone-break is *itself* great content ("watch it go soft the second I said I was hurt"). We never seed slurs, protected-class attacks, or the slap framed as real harm.

## 3. The launch MOMENT + the stunt

**The shareable moment — `roastme.forge`.** A single web demo, no install: type your real *why*, drag the **GENTLE → REAL → HARD → SAVAGE → UNHINGED** dial, and the coach roasts you on the spot (AI voice note at Savage/Unhinged). The output **is** the first viral artifact — ember-marked, one tap to share. **"Skip the waitlist by posting your roast"** turns every visitor into a distributor. This is pure top-of-funnel: the first viral act happens before anyone has trained a single rep.

**The stunt that earns the press cycle — the "Roast Booth."** A consented popup (creator house, college campus, gym lobby): real people walk up, hand the coach their *why*, and get slapped through the screen on camera while a live waitlist counter ticks behind them. The footage is the trailer. The headline writes itself:

> *"We built the app every other company is too scared to ship."*

Consent is signed at the booth, it's 18+, the avatar slaps the lens (never a person), and the injury tone-break is demoed on camera — so the "isn't this harmful?" rebuttal is in the public record before a critic asks.

## 4. Amplifying organic winners with paid

We do **not** buy reach cold. Organic picks the winners; paid pours fuel only on what already earns shares.

- **The whitelist rule.** Any organic post crossing a velocity threshold — **50k views in 24h, or shares/view above baseline** — gets **Spark-Ad'd** through the *creator's own handle*, keeping the native feel. A clip that didn't go viral on its own never gets a dollar.
- **Retarget the warm.** Demo-visitors and waitlist non-converters get the **"it actually works"** counterweight (day-7 progress, the guided rep, the injury backoff) — not the shock clip that brought them.
- **Throttle discipline (enforced, not aspirational).** If installs soar but **first-session completion drops below 55%** or the day-7 progress moment stops firing, **cut spend and fix the guided rep.** Aggression fills the funnel; the rep is what we refuse to let disappoint.

## 5. PR + controversy strategy — court the edge, avoid the blowup

The press story is **"responsibly savage"** — the restraint is the headline, not just the rage. *"The most aggressive app on the App Store — and the most careful about it"* is more defensible and more interesting than "edgy."

**Lead with the floor.** Opt-in, one-tap kill switch, contempt for the *excuse* never the *person*, instant backoff on injury or genuine crisis, never a slur or protected class, nothing touching self-harm or eating disorders, 18+, top tiers soft-locked for flagged-anxious users until a few logged sessions.

**Court controversy without a true blowup.** Own the divisive surface ourselves (X, our own clips). Seed the "is this healthy?" debate *deliberately*, then reframe it with prepared founder talking points:

> *"It's aggression you chose, aimed at your excuse, killable in one tap, built for the person who's quit four times. It's mean to your excuses, never to you — and it drops the act the instant you're actually hurt."*

**Pitch angles:** the anti-wellness backlash story; "the AI that's mean to you on purpose" trend piece; the founder manifesto — *aggression is the only thing competitors can't tie.*

## 7. The repeatability requirements — what must be true for the machine to keep producing hits

Virality here isn't luck, but it isn't free either. The honest read: **steady-state organic k sits *below* 1** — the engine is paid-amplified between seeded windows and runs hot (k≈1) only *during* challenge windows. The job is to shrink the paid subsidy toward zero over 90 days. Three things must hold, or the machine degrades into a meme that dies — or a paid-acquisition business in a viral costume:

1. **Installs-per-share (c) is real, not assumed.** Break-even rests on **c ≈ 0.5** — 2.5–5× a passive screenshot. The **"generate your own roast" producer loop** (`/roast/[id]`: a viewer types a why, gets their own shareable roast carrying the install link) must actually move c, or the subsidy never shrinks.
2. **First-session completion holds at ≥55% under aggression.** The savage hook selects for skeptics and dunkers — the worst-activating cohort. If loud traffic activates below baseline, the bucket leaks faster than we fill it. The throttle (§4) must be enforced.
3. **The mix rotates toward receipts/vindication over 90 days.** **60% aggression / 25% receipts / 15% vindication** is the *opening* ratio. If the team can't manufacture personalization-led hits at volume (harder to film than a slap), it defaults to shock and caps out as a one-screenshot meme. The receipts are the only asset that compounds — they can't be cold-started and deepen per user.

**The one number that governs the machine:** **organic shares per active user (s)**, tied to installs-per-share (c) — not invites, not raw views. When `s × c × i ≥ 1` *outside* a seeded window, the engine is self-propagating and we throttle spend into product. Until then, content is a lever we pull, not a fire we lit.

## 8. The first highest-leverage experiment

**Measure installs-per-share (c) on the producer loop in isolation — before spending on anything else.**

- **Setup:** ship `roastme.forge` with the **"generate your own / skip the line by posting"** mechanic and per-link attribution.
- **Drive:** a fixed cohort of seeded shares (one Wave-1 creator is enough).
- **Measure:** installs produced **per share** — c, in the wild, not on a spreadsheet.

**Why this and nothing else first:** c is the load-bearing variable the entire `k ≥ 1` thesis stands on, and it is currently a **guess**. Hooks are cheap to fix; a broken coefficient is the business.

- **If c lands at 0.1–0.2** (typical passive-screenshot range): this is paid acquisition wearing a viral costume — reframe the plan and budget accordingly.
- **If c clears ~0.4:** the engine is real. Pour fuel.

**Test the multiplier, not the hook.** We know the rage gets posted; we don't know whether each post makes the next user. That number decides whether FORGE is a movement or a meme — and it's the cheapest thing on this list to learn.
