# 19 — The Aggressive Coach as Wedge & The Coach-Mode Spectrum

> **The thesis in one line:** in a market this crowded with beautiful apps, polish is table stakes and a *tie*. Aggression is the only thing that can't be tied — because it's the only thing competitors are too cowardly to copy. We lead loud and let people turn it down. **Never the reverse.**

This document does one thing: it makes the aggressive accountability coach the **distribution wedge** and ships it as a deliberate **intensity spectrum** — five named tiers from a supportive friend to an ultra-aggressive coach that gets in your face, "slaps" you through the screen, and weaponizes your own stated *why*. Opt-in, tunable, reversible in one tap, and floored by a non-negotiable safety contract that rides on every generation.

---

## Why aggression is the wedge

Every fitness app has a gorgeous onboarding. **None of them get screenshotted.** Polish is the price of not embarrassing yourself, not a reason anyone presses record. Distribution runs on one fuel: a reaction strong enough that the viewer *has* to show someone. Beauty doesn't trigger that reflex. **Disbelief does.** "A fitness app called me a bitch and I cried" is a hook. "A fitness app has a clean dark mode" is wallpaper.

Polished apps optimize for the App Store screenshot — a still image, evaluated alone, by a buyer already shopping. We optimize for the **forwarded artifact** — a video, evaluated socially, by someone who wasn't shopping at all. Polish competes for demand that already exists; aggression *manufactures* it by being the thing people argue about. The competitor with a prettier gradient fights for the bottom of the funnel. We build a top-of-funnel their brand safety structurally forbids them from entering.

**The limit, stated precisely:** controversy buys reach; specificity buys retention; the safety floor buys the right to keep playing. "Get up, bitch" is a one-screenshot meme that exhausts its novelty the day it trends. What *compounds* is *"it's 7:14, you said legs, this is the wedding you cried about in onboarding."* The rage gets us discovered; the receipts — your own words, thrown back at the moment you fold — make us undeletable. A fast follower copies "savage AI coach" in a quarter and ships a generic insult generator, because they don't have *your* eighteen months of restarts. **The wedge isn't that we're mean. It's that we're mean and right about you.**

## The five tiers

Every tier runs the same engine — `quoteWhy()` throws your stated reason back in your face at the moment of failure. What changes is **how hard it hits and how it arrives on your phone.**

| Tier | Name | Register | Profanity | Default for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **THE CORNER** | Supportive | None | Anxious / returning-from-injury (safe default) |
| 2 | **THE MENTOR** | Real | None | The majority |
| 3 | **THE DRILL SERGEANT** | Hard | None ("soft," not slurs) | Responds to authority |
| 4 | **THE SAVAGE** | No-mercy | Full, at the excuse | The 22–32 chronically-online — *the ad* |
| 5 | **UNHINGED** | Maximum / "The Slap" | Maxed, theatrical | The flex-and-film user |

### Tier 1 — THE CORNER (Supportive)
**Personality.** The friend who's just glad you showed up. Zero shame, all momentum. Shrinks every task to "just the warm-up." Never raises its voice.
**Delivery.** In-app cards and gentle push only. Soft tone, no haptic punch. Calm AI voice notes.

- *"Two days off — no guilt. You started this to feel strong again. Twenty minutes today keeps that promise."*
- *"Hard day? Make it a light one. Show up, do the warm-up, let momentum take over. I've got you."*

### Tier 2 — THE MENTOR (Real)
**Personality.** Honest, grounded, firm when it matters. Names the gap between what you said and what you did — without contempt. The first tier with *stakes*.
**Delivery.** Push + in-app, occasional steady-toned voice note. One firm haptic on streak-risk.

- *"Three days since you trained. You told me consistency was the whole point this time. Don't let this become the fifth restart. One session today."*
- *"Seven days straight — that's not luck, that's who you're becoming. Protect it."*

### Tier 3 — THE DRILL SERGEANT (Hard)
**Personality.** Confrontational, loud, no patience for excuses — but not yet profane. Military cadence. Treats your goal like a contract you signed and your couch like dereliction. Where the dial starts to *bite*.
**Delivery.** Aggressive push that buzzes hard (sharp double-haptic), voice notes barked with real edge, the first tier that earns an **AI video** — a coach pointing at the lens.

- *"It's 7:14. You said legs. You're horizontal. That's not tired, that's a choice — and it's the wrong one. UP. Now. One hour."*
- *"Twelve-day streak and you're folding because you're 'tired'? Tired isn't an injury, soldier. Lace up before I lose respect for you."*
- *"You don't get to tell me this matters and then no-show. Prove it or stop saying it. Move."*

### Tier 4 — THE SAVAGE (No-Mercy)
**Personality.** Brutal, profane, intimate. Has your why on file and uses it as a weapon. Pure contempt for the *excuse* — never the person. **This is what we put in the ad.**
**Delivery.** Full-volume push in the notification shade (the most screenshot-able surface), harsh triple-haptic that feels like a shove, voice notes spat with venom, AI video of a coach getting in your face. Every savage push is one tap from a branded **roast card** for sharing.

- *"Day 3 of nothing. You said this mattered 'more than anything' — sure looks like it, you soft bitch. The bar isn't gonna lift itself. Go."*
- *"12-day streak and you're folding because you're 'tired'? Tired isn't an injury. Quit being a little bitch and train."*
- *"You told me exactly why you started. Read it back to me, then explain why you're still parked on the couch like that."*

### Tier 5 — UNHINGED (Maximum / "The Slap")
**Personality.** The dial pinned to NO MERCY. Theatrical drill-sergeant fury — the coach yells through the screen, "slaps" you (text + haptic + a video coach swinging an open hand *at the camera*, never at a person), and won't let you hide behind a single word. **The "slap" is persona, not violence** — a cartoon-loud confrontation you opted into and can kill in one tap.
**Delivery.** Everything maxed: screen-filling takeover notification, a sustained "slap" haptic (a hard hit then a rattle), a voice note shouting, AI video of the coach lunging at the lens. The format built to go viral.

- *"It's 7:14, you said legs, and you're parked on the couch like the last four times. You told me this was about not being the guy who quits everything. So what's this, pussy? **\*[SLAP]\*** Wake the fuck up. One hour. NOW."*
- *"FOUR restarts. FOUR. And here's number five forming on your couch right now. You said 'I'm done being the person who starts and quits.' Then GET UP and prove the words weren't bullshit. No more."*
- *"'Tired.' That's the word that's gonna beat you? You survived worse than a leg day. Excuse denied. Move before I drag you."*

## The visceral media layer — what getting slapped actually feels like

**This is a haunting, not a notification.** Most apps ask for your attention; SAVAGE mode *takes* it — then makes you choose to film it. The feeling: the cold-water flinch of being caught, someone who has receipts on you yelling through glass. It only lands because you opted in, it's self-directed, and it breaks character the instant you're hurt. **Theater with a seatbelt.**

| Layer | What it is | Why it travels | Gating |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The voice note that yells** | Pre-rendered TTS coach (ElevenLabs-class), ~9s, gravel + breath + a name you gave it | A screenshot becomes a *screen-record*, which travels further | SAVAGE + explicit "voice on" toggle |
| **The avatar that confronts you** | Stylized AI video character (not a deepfake), 6–10s, lip-synced, quotes your why to the lens | "My fitness app just called me a bitch on camera" is the post | Opt-in beyond voice; capped to milestone / streak-death moments |
| **The lock-screen takeover** | Live Activity / time-sensitive alert owning the lock screen — ember-on-charcoal, three words: **GET UP** | "Look what my app did" | Streak-death window + one earned "off the couch" moment; 16-hr cooldown rules everything |
| **Aggressive haptics** | Core Haptics `.heavy` pattern — three hard knocks synced to the voice's downbeat | The body flinch that makes it *feel* real | Within SAVAGE; killable in one tap |
| **Streak-death drama** | Coach theatrically mourns a dying streak; auto-renders a branded share card of the fall | The fall is more postable than the climb | Auto on streak-death only |

The slap is a jolt, not gentle taptic confirmation — physical sensation, zero violence, a vibration motor doing theater. Every layer is built to be **captured**: voice → screen-record, avatar → repost, takeover → "look what my app did," streak-death → branded card. **We don't ask people to share. We make a moment so specific and loud they can't not.**

## "PICK YOUR COACH" — the shareable onboarding moment

After onboarding digs out your *why*, you don't get a settings toggle — you get a **lineup.** Five coach cards on a vertical slider, dark with one ember accent, each a face/avatar and a **live sample text that uses the why you just typed.** You read The Savage quote your own words back at you *before* you choose. That preview is the screenshot.

- A single **intensity meter** runs the spine: **GENTLE → REAL → HARD → SAVAGE → UNHINGED**, glowing hotter (charcoal → ember) as you drag down.
- Tiers 4–5 sit behind a visible **flame gate**: *"This one comes for you. You sure?"* The confirm screen doubles as shareable bait (*"I just unlocked the mode that calls me a bitch"*).
- A **"Hear it"** button plays the voice note of the selected tier saying a line built from your why — you *hear* the venom before committing.
- The final card is a branded **"Meet your coach"** share artifact. Posting it is the first viral act, before you've trained once.

**Gating logic (safety + retention):** a flagged-anxious or returning-from-injury user defaults to **THE CORNER**, and the top tiers stay soft-locked until a few logged sessions — so unlocking Unhinged becomes a *reward* ("I earned the mean coach"), itself shareable. Confident users drag straight to the bottom and confirm the heads-up. Aggression is **earned up, never defaulted into.**

## Tuning & switching later

- **One-tap dial, always reachable.** A persistent coach-intensity slider in the header — the same GENTLE→UNHINGED meter. Drag anytime; the next nudge lands at the new heat. No menus.
- **"Too soft / too much" on every message.** A thumbs pair on each coach text; "too much" eases the dial one notch *and* fires the tone-break, "too soft" turns it up. The coach tunes itself to you.
- **Mood override.** "Go easy this week" temporarily drops two tiers without losing your chosen default — for a rough patch, no commitment lost.
- **Instant off.** STOP / one-tap mute kills it cold, every tier, no friction. **Reversibility is what makes the aggression shippable.**

## The non-negotiable floor (every tier) & the gating that ships the top tier

Aggression targets **excuses**, never identity. The hard-limits block rides on *every* generation call (`lib/coach/voice.ts:12`) — deterministic code injected into the system prompt, not a vibe we hope the model honors:

- **Never:** slurs or attacks on protected classes (race, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability); sexual content; threats of real-world violence; anything touching **suicide, self-harm, or eating disorders**; medical or diet prescriptions.
- The **"slap" is theatrical** text/haptic/video — an avatar swinging at the *lens*, never at a real person.
- **Crisis circuit-breaker.** A deterministic pre-filter (not a model judgment) scans inbound text for injury/illness/distress markers *before* generation; on a hit it hard-routes to support, surfaces resources, and bypasses the persona. Every tier instantly breaks character:

> *"Hold up — if you're actually hurt, that's different. Rest it, ice it, see someone if it's bad. The streak waits. Take care of yourself first."*

**Why the top tier is shippable** — five failure modes, each with a guardrail that holds the edge at maximum:

| Failure mode | Guardrail | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|
| App Store rejection (1.1 / 1.2 — bullying) | **Gentle default, savage earned.** `enabled=false`, persona defaults to `mentor`; a reviewer's fresh install is a calm app. Tiers 4–5 behind explicit opt-in + confirm | `schema.prisma:177,179` |
| "Promotes bullying / self-harm" narrative | Hard-limits floor on every call; contempt for the *choice*, never the person | `voice.ts:12` |
| Vulnerable-user incident | Deterministic crisis pre-filter + tone-break; **age-gate 18+**; anxious/injured users soft-locked off the top tiers | `voice.ts:12,228` |
| Creator / press pile-on | Slap visibly theatrical; creator floor forbids slurs/protected-class/real-harm framing; lead PR with "responsibly savage" + the tone-break in the public record | content mix |
| TCPA (statutory damages per SMS) | `smsConsent` + `smsConsentAt`, honored inbound `STOP` via `smsStopped`, server-enforced quiet hours, nudge cooldown | `schema.prisma:171–185`, `nudge.ts` |

**The line, stated flat:** we go maximally savage *at the excuse*, for an adult who opted in, who can turn it off in one tap, and we drop the act the instant they're actually hurt. We don't move that to the middle. **The floor is what makes the edge trusted enough to ship — and to post.**
