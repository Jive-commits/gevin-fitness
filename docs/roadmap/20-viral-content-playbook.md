# 20 — The CMO Viral Content Playbook: The Machine That Manufactures Virality

> **Thesis.** Virality isn't luck, it's a factory. Aggression is the raw material; specificity is the finishing process; the screenshot is the product coming off the line. We are not hoping a video pops — we run a press that stamps out the same shape (disbelief → "wait, it *knows* me" → forward) at a fixed cadence until the For You page can't avoid us. **Stop starting over** is the brand line. *"A fitness app called me a bitch and I cried"* is the bait. This document is filmable **this week.**

The wedge is the aggressive coach, and the aggression is what we *market with* — not the thing we hide behind a gentle default. Polish is a tie; aggression can't be tied, because competitors are too cowardly to copy it. Below is the manufacturing system: hook taxonomy, 16 ready-to-shoot concepts, the founder cadence, account architecture, the UGC engine, creator seeding, the controversy dial, and the content-to-install funnel.

## 1. The Hook Taxonomy — the 5 shapes that work for THIS brand

Every video is one of five archetypes. Don't invent a sixth — fill these.

| # | Hook | Example line | Job | Reach / Retention |
|---|------|--------------|-----|-------------------|
| 1 | **Disbelief** | "A fitness app just called me a bitch." | Pure controversy. Buys the impression. | Highest reach / low retention |
| 2 | **Receipts** | "I told it ONE thing in onboarding. Watch what it did with it." | Controversy as on-ramp, personalization as payload. The compounding shape. | Mid / highest |
| 3 | **Dare** | "I set the coach to its meanest mode. I regret everything." | Self-inflicted; makes the viewer want to do it too. The dial *is* the content. | High / mid |
| 4 | **Reaction** | "I wasn't ready for what it said back." | Face-cam, real flinch. The body doesn't lie. | Mid / high |
| 5 | **Callout / Vindication** | "Every fitness app is too scared to do this." / "It called me lazy and it was RIGHT." | Picks the fight, or admits the app won — the turn that converts haters. | Mid / converts |

**Rule:** Disbelief and Dare get you *found*. Receipts and Reaction make you *undeletable*. Never post a week that's all Disbelief — that's how you become a one-screenshot meme that dies.

## 3. Founder-as-Creator + Cadence

The founder is the cheapest, highest-trust creator we have — a real person who built the mean app beats any paid creator. Posture: **the slightly unhinged builder who made the app that bullies him.**

- **Format:** "I built a fitness app to bully me into the gym. Here's what it said today." Daily build-in-public + daily roast. The founder is the recurring character; the app is the co-star.
- **Cadence:** **1 post/day minimum, every platform, 90 days non-negotiable.** Volume is the one algorithm input you control. Most flop; you're farming the 1-in-15 that hits.
- **Workflow (≤30 min/day):** batch-film 5–7 raw clips in one Sunday session → keep a running Notes list of the app's *actual* savage texts (the product writes your scripts) → post the day's best, native to each platform.
- **Unfair advantage:** post the *real* texts the app sends *you*. Authenticity can't be faked, and competitors literally can't reproduce your eighteen months of why.

## 4. Account Architecture

Don't run one account. Run a portfolio so the algorithm has many shots and you can swing edgier on accounts that aren't the brand.

| Account | Role | Content | Dial | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **@forge** (main) | Brand face | Restrained-edgy, leads with the wedge, *visibly* shows the floor (concept 16 lives here) | **6/10** | What reviewers and press see. Polished, ember accent. |
| **@founder** (creator) | Build-in-public + daily roast | Highest trust, most volume | **8/10** | Unhinged because it's *him*, not the brand. |
| **3–5 alt "fan" accounts** (the lab) | Risk firewall + A/B surface | Faceless meme accounts ("meancoachclips," "thatappcalledmeabitch"), POV templates, slap clips | **9–10/10** | A strike here never touches the brand. Winners get reposted to main. |
| **15–30 seeded creators** (50k–500k) | UGC at scale | Unscripted — the product roasts *them* on camera | Their own | Provide the device and the moment, not the words. |

**UGC engine.** The in-app roast card and streak-death card are the manufacturing line — every active user is a potential creator. The "I unlocked the mean coach" share artifact at onboarding means the **first viral act happens before the user has trained once.** Measure organic shares/active user, not invites.

## 5. The UGC / Creator Engine — artifacts, challenges, seeding

**Every aggressive moment must terminate in a forwardable object, not just a feeling.** We never *ask* anyone to share — we engineer moments so loud and specific they can't not.

### Shareable in-app artifacts

| Artifact | Trigger | Why it begs to be posted |
|---|---|---|
| **Roast Card** | SAVAGE/UNHINGED nudge fires; "Screenshot this" surfaces once, post-moment | Quotes your own why in the meanest voice. PII-stripped, ember mark. The original viral unit. |
| **Voice-Note Drop** | Same trigger, "voice on"; built-in screen-record + share | A text is read; a voice is *experienced* → travels further on TikTok. |
| **Avatar Confrontation** | Milestone / streak-death only (capped, so it stays an event) | "My fitness app called me a bitch on camera." Stylized character = reads as theater, not deepfake. |
| **Streak-Death Card** | Built streak hits Day 0 | The fall is more postable than the climb. Auto-renders the receipt. |
| **Streak-Flex Card** | Milestones {7,14,30,50,100} | A humblebrag *with a punchline*. The counterweight that proves it works. |
| **Weekly Recap Card** | Sunday cron | The roast is the spike; the recap is the **heartbeat** — doesn't depend on a failure firing. |
| **Meet Your Coach Card** | End of "PICK YOUR COACH" | The first viral act, before training once. |

**Two-way formats.** Every card has a caption-safe top band sized for a **stitch/duet** — we design the *crop* for the remix. **"Rate my coach roast"** (`/roast/[id]`, no auth): viewers score a roast 1–10 and *generate their own* from a typed why — the viewer becomes a producer in one tap. Design rule: every card must look good **muted and cropped**, because that's how it travels.

### Named challenges

- **#CoachRoastChallenge (flagship).** (1) Read your real onboarding why aloud — the vulnerable setup; (2) reveal the coach's roast — the violent punchline; (3) tag + your reaction. Arc = *vulnerability → violence → relatability.* We seed a sound (the AI "GET UP" downbeat) so every entry is discoverable and stitchable.
- **#LastFirstDay (redemption counter-challenge).** Post your Day-0 card, then post the streak-flex over it 30 days later. Structurally retention-positive — you can't complete it without coming back.
- **#DareItToGoMax.** Film dragging the dial to UNHINGED, confirming the heads-up, reacting to the slap. The dial *is* the content.

### Creator-seeding playbook

- **Tier A — edgy fitness/comedy, 50k–500k.** Sweet spot: big enough to move installs, small enough the reaction reads as real. Gym-bro comedy, reaction/duet, chronically-online lanes — **not** aesthetic-transformation accounts.
- **Tier B — non-fitness reaction/POV.** The mean coach is funny to people who don't lift; expands past the gym audience.
- **Tier C — 1–2 marquee names** for a launch spike, *only after* Tier A proves the format converts.
- **Offer:** small base + per-install/per-roast bounty + early UNHINGED access (the unlock is their content). The payout *is* a growth loop.
- **Brief (anti-script):** *"Tell it the most honest reason you want this. Hit record and let it come for you. Don't write jokes — the specificity is the joke. Show the dial. Show your face when the voice plays. If it goes too far, that reaction is the best part — keep it in."*
- **Creator floor (protects the channel):** creators must show it's opt-in, self-directed, and that it backs off — the injury tone-break is itself great content ("watch it go soft the second I said I was hurt").

## 6. The Controversy Dial — per platform

- **Trend-jack rule:** the app's *reaction* is the meme template. When a sound trends, the savage text or slap video is the punchline you drop onto it. You don't chase the trend — you make the app the unexpected payload.

| Platform | Dial | Effort | Native unit | Play |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | **8/10** | 60% | Screen-record of the slap / 9-sec voice note | Lead loud. **Bleep the harshest profanity in-frame** — a censor bar reads *edgier* and dodges auto-moderation. Post 3–5x/day across the roster. Chase **shares**, not likes. |
| **Reels** | **6/10** | 25% | Branded roast / streak-death still | Saves + sends. Lean Receipts/Reaction over raw Disbelief; censor more. Carousels of the coach-mode lineup are save-bait. |
| **Shorts** | **5/10** | 10% | Vindication + "it works" reaction arc | Most conservative moderation. Cleanest cuts; concept 9 + day-7 progress earn their keep. Longest tail. |
| **X / Twitter** | owned | 5% | The most divisive clips, posted by us | Not for reach — for the **argument**. Court the edge under our own control where we moderate the floor live. |

> **Hard floor everywhere (non-negotiable — this is what keeps us shippable).** Aggression aims at the *excuse*, never the person. **Never:** slurs or attacks on protected classes (race, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability); sexual content; threats of real-world violence; anything touching suicide, self-harm, or eating disorders. The "slap" is always visibly theatrical — a stylized avatar at the lens, never a real person. The coach **backs off instantly** on injury, illness, or genuine crisis. Cross the line and you don't get a viral video, you get deplatformed — and the floor is *also* the trust that makes people willing to post.

## 7. The Content-to-Install Funnel

1. **Hook (0–3s):** disbelief or dare — buys the watch.
2. **Payload (3–10s):** the personalization reveal — your why quoted back. The turn from "lol" to "wait, I need this."
3. **Proof (optional, 10–12s):** day-7 number moved, or the tone-break on injury — converts the skeptic.
4. **CTA — soft, never "download now":** the video IS the ad; the comments are the conversion layer. Pin: *"it's called FORGE if you hate yourself enough 💀"* → link in bio → **waitlist landing with the live roast generator** (the sample text is the visitor's first shareable artifact) → capture phone for SMS → "skip the line by posting your roast."
5. **Loop closure:** new user onboards → picks their coach (first viral act) → gets roasted → in-app share card → posts → back to step 1.

**Paid discipline.** We do **not** buy reach cold. Any organic post crossing a velocity threshold (≈50k views / 24h, or shares-per-view above baseline) gets **whitelisted/Spark-Ad'd through the creator's own handle** so it keeps the native feel. A clip that didn't earn shares never gets a dollar.

**The governing metric** — not views, not installs — is **organic shares per active user (target 0.30+, ramp 0.15 → 0.40)**, tied to **first-session completion (≥55%)**. If shares spike but completion sags, we've manufactured a meme, not a movement — throttle the loud accounts and feed the vindication/"it works" concepts until the bucket holds. The viral identity: **k = s × c × i ≥ 1** self-propagates; outside seeded windows the engine is paid-amplified, and the 90-day job is to push *c* up (producer loops) and concentrate *s* (challenges) so the paid subsidy shrinks toward zero.

---

**The machine in one line:** a fixed roster of 5 hook shapes, a portfolio of accounts that lets us swing 9/10 without risking the brand, a founder posting daily from the app's own real output, an in-product share line that turns every roasted user into the next creator — all pointed at one number (shares/active user) and floored by a safety line that's the reason people trust it enough to post. **We don't hope for viral. We stamp it out. Stop starting over.**
